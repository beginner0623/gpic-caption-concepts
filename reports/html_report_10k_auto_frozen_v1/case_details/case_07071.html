The top of a page from an old book shows ornate black ink decorations above large printed text. The title reads “A TRESHAVT ET PVISSANT SEIGNEUR,” followed by the name “HENRY DE LA TOVR, Marechal de France.” Below that, smaller text lists titles such as “Duc de Bouillon, Seigneur fouverain, Vicomte de Turrenne, Comte de Montfort, Baron de Mongaçon, Olierges, Fay, Seruiffac, Croq, &c.” A decorative header includes a central face with a crown flanked by floral and scroll-like patterns. The page has a slightly worn texture and is printed in a serif font typical of historical documents. At the very top right corner, the number “3” is visible, likely indicating a page number. The entire scene is in grayscale, suggesting it’s a reproduction or scan of an aged manuscript.
