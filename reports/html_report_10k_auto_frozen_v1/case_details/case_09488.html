A stone lion statue with detailed, flowing mane and open mouth is carved onto a decorative architectural element. The lion sits atop an ornate capital that features leaf-like carvings and rests on a thick stone pillar. To the left of the lion, a twisted rope-like carving runs vertically along the wall, and to its right, the rough surface of a large stone wall is visible. The lion’s head is turned slightly to the side, and its paws are positioned on a flat stone ledge. Behind the lion, part of a carved stone panel with floral patterns can be seen, while below it, the base of the column shows signs of weathering. The entire structure is made of light gray stone with a textured, uneven surface.
