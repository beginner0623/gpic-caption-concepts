A yellow plastic box contains twelve small, oval-shaped pastries arranged in four rows of three, each individually wrapped in clear plastic. The top surface of each pastry has a dark brown Japanese character stamped into it, and some have a slightly darker, caramelized center. A white paper insert with Japanese text and illustrations is partially tucked under the bottom right corner of the box. The text on the paper includes phrases like “お菓子” (sweets) and “開封後は2日” (after opening, within 2 days), along with small cartoon faces and a warning symbol. The pastries appear soft and slightly golden-brown, with a glossy finish from the plastic wrapping. The box lid is open, revealing the organized layout of the treats inside, and the background shows a plain, light-colored surface beneath the box. The overall presentation suggests a packaged gift or snack item designed for consumption shortly after opening.
